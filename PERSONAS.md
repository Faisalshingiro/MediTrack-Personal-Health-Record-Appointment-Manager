# Human-Centered Design: User Personas

## Persona 1: Sarah, The Busy Patient
- **Role:** Patient
- **Age:** 34
- **Needs:** Quick booking, ability to track past visits without calling the clinic, clear interface for mobile use.
- **Pain Points:** Hard to keep track of medical notes across different doctors.
- **How MEDI-TRACK solves it:** The patient-specific dashboard allows Sarah to book appointments instantly and add her own "Personal Health Notes" to keep a digital journal of her symptoms.

## Persona 2: Dr. Robert, The Senior Physician
- **Role:** Doctor
- **Age:** 58
- **Needs:** Simple list of today's patients, easy way to record diagnosis without complex menus.
- **Pain Points:** Distracted by cluttered UIs; needs to focus on the patient.
- **How MEDI-TRACK solves it:** The Doctor Dashboard provides a focused table of pending/approved appointments. A single click opens a clean modal for diagnosis entry, reducing cognitive load.

## Design Decisions Explanation
1. **Dark Theme:** High contrast and reduced eye strain for healthcare workers working long shifts.
2. **Dashboard Layout:** Consistent sidebar navigation ensures the user always knows where they are (Spatial consistency).
3. **Modal Dialogs:** Keep users in context. Instead of navigating away to book or add notes, stay on the dashboard for better flow.
4. **Role Tags:** Visible role badges in the header reassure users of their current permissions.
